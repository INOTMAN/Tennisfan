The Big Four

Grand Slam Profiles

Hard Court

Australian Open

📍 Melbourne Park, Melbourne, Australia

🏆

The first Grand Slam of the year, held under the summer sun of Melbourne every January. Known for its scorching heat and the famous Plexicushion surface, the Australian Open provides a fast, consistent bounce that rewards aggressive baseliners.

Founded 1905
Capacity 15,000 (Rod Laver)
Surface Plexicushion (Hard)
Prize Money ~$86M AUD

Recent Champions

2025 Men'sJ. Sinner 🇮🇹
2025 Women'sA. Sabalenka 🇧🇾
2024 Men'sJ. Sinner 🇮🇹
2024 Women'sA. Sabalenka 🇧🇾
Clay Court

Roland Garros

📍 Stade Roland Garros, Paris, France

🏆

The French Open is the most physically demanding Grand Slam. Played on red crushed brick clay, the surface slows the ball down significantly, creating long, grueling rallies. It demands supreme fitness, mental toughness, and heavy topspin. Rafael Nadal's 14 titles here remain one of the most incredible records in sport.

Founded 1891
Capacity 15,059 (Phillipe-Chatrier)
Surface Red Clay
Prize Money ~€53M

Recent Champions

2024 Men'sC. Alcaraz 🇪🇸
2024 Women'sI. Swiatek 🇵🇱
2023 Men'sN. Djokovic 🇷🇸
2023 Women'sI. Swiatek 🇵🇱
Grass Court

Wimbledon

📍 The All England Club, London, United Kingdom

🏆

The oldest and most prestigious Grand Slam in tennis. Founded in 1877, Wimbledon is synonymous with tradition — white clothing, strawberries and cream, and the reverence of the Royal Box. The natural grass surface produces fast, low bounces, making it the ultimate test for serve-and-volley specialists.

Founded 1877
Capacity 15,000 (Centre Court)
Surface Ryegrass
Prize Money ~£50M

Recent Champions

2024 Men'sC. Alcaraz 🇪🇸
2024 Women'sB. Krejcikova 🇨🇿
2023 Men'sC. Alcaraz 🇪🇸
2023 Women'sM. Vondrousova 🇨🇿
Hard Court

US Open

📍 USTA Billie Jean King National Tennis Center, New York, USA

🏆

The final Grand Slam of the year, played under the lights of New York City. The US Open is famous for its electric, vocal crowd and its fast DecoTurf hard court surface. Night sessions under the Arthur Ashe Stadium lights are among the most iconic moments in all of tennis.

Founded 1881
Capacity 23,771 (Arthur Ashe)
Surface DecoTurf (Hard)
Prize Money ~$75M USD

Recent Champions

2024 Men'sJ. Sinner 🇮🇹
2024 Women'sA. Sabalenka 🇧🇾
2023 Men'sN. Djokovic 🇷🇸
2023 Women'sC. Gauff 🇺🇸
